DC Cable Diameter Calculator

Calculate required cable cross-sectional area and diameter for DC electrical systems

Supports 12V up to 60V DC systems

Input Parameters

Enter system voltage in volts, maximum 60V
Enter current in amperes
Enter cable length in meters
Optional: Maximum allowed voltage drop percentage, default is 2%

Calculation Formula

Required cable cross-sectional area:
\[A = \frac{I \times \rho(T_{eff}) \times L \times d}{\Delta V_{max}}\]

Temperature Conversion

\[T(°F) = T(°C) \times \frac{9}{5} + 32\]
Fahrenheit to Celsius:
\[T(°C) = (T(°F) - 32) \times \frac{5}{9}\]

Resistivity at Temperature

\[\rho(T) = \rho_{25°C} \times (1 + \alpha \times (T - T_{ref}))\]
\(\rho(T)\): Resistivity at temperature T (\(\Omega \cdot mm^2/m\))
\(\rho_{25°C}\): Reference resistivity at 25°C (\(\Omega \cdot mm^2/m\))
\(\alpha\): Temperature coefficient (\(/°C\))
\(T\): Temperature (°C)
\(T_{ref}\): Reference temperature (25°C)
Learn more: WolframAlpha | Wikipedia

Effective Operating Temperature

\[T_{eff} = T_{ambient} + \Delta T_{installation}\]
\(T_{eff}\): Effective operating temperature (°C)
\(T_{ambient}\): Ambient temperature (°C)
\(\Delta T_{installation}\): Installation temperature adjustment (°C)
Air: 0°C, Conduit: +10°C, Isolated: +20°C
Learn more: Wikipedia

Required Cable Cross-Sectional Area

\[A = \frac{I \times \rho(T_{eff}) \times L \times d}{\Delta V_{max}}\]
\(A\): Cross-sectional area (\(mm^2\))
\(I\): Current (A)
\(\rho(T_{eff})\): Resistivity at effective temperature (\(\Omega \cdot mm^2/m\))
\(L\): Cable length (m)
\(d\): Distance factor (1 for one-way, 2 for round trip)
\(\Delta V_{max}\): Maximum voltage drop (V)
Learn more: WolframAlpha | Wikipedia

Diameter from Cross-Sectional Area

\[d = 2 \times \sqrt{\frac{A}{\pi}}\]
\(d\): Diameter (mm)
\(A\): Cross-sectional area (\(mm^2\))
Learn more: WolframAlpha | Wikipedia

Cable Weight

\[m = A \times L \times \rho_{material} \times n\]
\(m\): Total cable weight (g)
\(A\): Cross-sectional area (\(mm^2\))
\(L\): Cable length (m)
\(\rho_{material}\): Material density (g/m per mm²)
\(n\): Number of conductors (1 for one-way, 2 for round trip)
Learn more: WolframAlpha | Wikipedia

Voltage Drop

\[\Delta V = \frac{I \times \rho(T_{eff}) \times L \times d}{A}\]
\(\Delta V\): Voltage drop (V)
\(I\): Current (A)
\(\rho(T_{eff})\): Resistivity at effective temperature (\(\Omega \cdot mm^2/m\))
\(L\): Cable length (m)
\(d\): Distance factor (1 for one-way, 2 for round trip)
\(A\): Cross-sectional area (\(mm^2\))
